## The accent rule

Two colours carry meaning. Everything else is ink and surface.

- **Green `#699900` is the primary accent.** It marks actions and interactions
  the agronomist owns: the primary CTA, `Start service`, text links, the
  active tab, the active day, the selected state.
- **Blue `#0056B8` is the second accent, and it is narrow.** It marks
  system-owned moments only: the in-progress state of a running service (its
  chip, its card wash, its elapsed timer) and the `Complete` action that
  finalises the service.
- Green starts a service; blue is the system running it and finalising it.
  **Nothing else is blue** â€” not links, not decoration, not a generic
  highlight.

One filled green action per screen is the anchor and leads the eye. Links and
selected states are green too, but quieter (green text, a `green-pale` wash),
so the filled action still wins.

Red and amber are **semantic urgency only**, not brand. Red is time-window
risk; amber is unknown or first touch (and carries the "service incomplete"
state).

## Anti-patterns

Do not ship a screen with:

- A **coloured card edge** on any side to mark a category. Use an eyebrow
  with an 8px dot instead â€” dot colour is the only colour on the card
  (green for opportunity or signal, amber for risk, red for critical).
- **More than one accent stacked on one element** (tint plus border plus
  icon-circle). Pick one.
- A **second shadow**, or Material elevation. The DS ships one soft shadow.
- **Gradients, sparkles, rainbow-numbered lists, or emoji used as icons.**
- A **hero that is a gradient plus emoji plus a three-feature grid.** Lead
  with a stat or a line.

The self-test: *would Stripe or Linear ship this?* If no, simplify.

## Copy

Plain writing. Short sentences, one idea each.

- **No em-dashes.**
- **No inflated words** (`leverage`, `robust`, `seamless`, `comprehensive`,
  `navigate`, `crucial`).
- **No line that announces what a section is.** Just show the section.
- Write "Section 4", never the section symbol (`Â§`).
- Do not flag copy in the screen â€” no `placeholder`, no `sample text`, no
  `Legal/DP dependency` labels on the mock. Write the real string as if it
  were final. Open questions live in the tracker, never on the screen.

## Errors

One error surface: a toast at the bottom, with a single exception.

**Field error (the one exception).** A red helper line under the field,
with a small `alert-triangle` icon, in `red-text`. It replaces the field's
normal helper while the error holds, because it belongs to that field.
`Input` and `Textarea` render this automatically when you set `error`.

**Everything else is a toast at the bottom.** A sign-in that fails, an
account that is not authorised, a dropped network. One treatment, one
place. The only difference is how long it stays:

- A **blocking** error (for example "Your account is not authorised. Please
  contact your administrator.") stays long enough to read â€” around six
  seconds or until tapped. Pass `autoHideMs={6000}` on `Toast`.
- A **transient, retryable** one (for example "Unable to sign in. Please
  try again.") auto-dismisses in three or four seconds â€” `autoHideMs`
  defaults to `4000`.

Toasts have one style, always. **Dark ink pill, white text, pinned to the
bottom, at most one text action** (for example "Retry") in a light tint. Do
not colour a toast by type. **A toast is never red and never green:** the
copy carries the meaning. This holds for a success toast ("Saved") as much
as for an error one.

Top placement is for a persistent system banner (offline, a new version) â€”
that is `Banner`, not `Toast`. Different component, different rules.
